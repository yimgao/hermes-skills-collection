1/8 🧵 I analyzed 9 US cities to find the best location for a ramen + dumpling shop.
The winner surprised me. 🍜

2/8 I scored each city on 5 dimensions:
• Market demand (Asian %, income, population)
• Competition gap (# of ramen shops)
• Affordability (rent, startup costs)
• Foot traffic (walkability, tourism)
• Growth (population, new developments)

3/8 The results:
🥇 Raleigh-Durham, NC — 8.5/10
🥈 Columbus, OH — 8.0/10
🥉 Charlotte, NC — 7.8/10
4️⃣ Austin, TX — 7.5/10
5️⃣ Salt Lake City, UT — 7.3/10

4/8 Why Raleigh-Durham?
• Asian population grew 60% in 5 years (now ~8%)
• Apple & Google building huge campuses → bringing CA workers who crave ramen
• Only ONE ramen shop in downtown Durham — rated 4.2⭐

5/8 The biggest gap?
Every competitor closes by 9:30-10PM.
But Durham Performing Arts Center (2,600 seats) lets out at 9-10PM with nowhere to eat.
Zero late-night ramen options. 🚀

6/8 Financial model for a Ramen + Gyoza Bar:
• Startup: $280K
• Break-even: Month 5-6
• Year 1 revenue: $680K → profit: $88K
• Year 3 revenue: $1.1M → profit: $176K

7/8 Bottom line: Downtown Durham needs a ramen bar that stays open until 1AM, serves handmade noodles + gyoza, and gives people a place to eat after the theater.
